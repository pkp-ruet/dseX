// FAQ
What is the dseX Score?
The dseX Score (0–100) ranks every Dhaka Stock Exchange company on 8 fundamental factors across 4 groups: Valuation (35%), Profitability (25%), Dividend Quality (25%), and Balance Sheet Safety (15%). Each factor is percentile-ranked — a score of 80 means that company beats 80% of the market on these fundamentals.
Is this a buy/sell recommendation?
No. dseX is a screening and research tool, not financial advice. The score reflects historical fundamental data scraped from DSE. Always do your own research before investing.
How often is the data updated?
The scrapers run periodically to fetch new prices, financials, dividends, and shareholding data from the DSE website. The Streamlit app caches data for 5 minutes. Financial data (annual reports) is updated when DSE publishes new figures.
Which companies are included?
All non-excluded companies listed on the Dhaka Stock Exchange (DSE), across all sectors — bank, insurance, pharma, textile, cement, food, telecom, engineering, and more. Category Z companies receive a score multiplier of 0.30 to reflect higher risk.
How is the data collected?
Custom Python scrapers pull data directly from the DSE website and store it in MongoDB Atlas across 5 collections: companies, stock_prices, financials, shareholdings, and company_news. The scoring engine then processes this data to generate the dseX Score for each company.
Is the source code available?
Yes. The full source code is open on GitHub at github.com/pkp-ruet/dseX.
